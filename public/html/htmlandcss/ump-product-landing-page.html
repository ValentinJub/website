The doctrine of the UMP declares that it is necessary to give free rein to the "individual destiny of the person" which goes beyond "social determinism". The UMP "rejects systems that stifle (economic) freedom by trying to control everything". Work, merit and innovation must be encouraged in order to lead France towards renewed growth and lower unemployment. However, this freedom should not be understood as selfishness: "Freedom opens the way to generosity". To ensure that this freedom is respected, strict respect for the law is necessary: "The authority of the State and the justice system must ensure that everyone is accountable for their actions.

The UMP declares itself very attached to solidarity: "the State is there to guarantee the social protection to which the French are legitimately attached". Nevertheless, solidarity must "respect the individual" and "not (...) turn into assistance". It is essentially limited to the fields of public services, equality between territories, support for families and the school of the Republic, which must "allow those who are distinguished by their merits, to exercise the highest responsibilities".
